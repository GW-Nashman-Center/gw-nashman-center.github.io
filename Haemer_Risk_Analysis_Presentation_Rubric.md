# Haemer Risk Analysis Presentation Rubric

- Presentation: Spokesman for each group will be expected to present the group’s conclusions as if it were being presented at a professional conference using presentation techniques that the student believes will maximize the effectiveness of the presentation.


# Oral Presentation Grading Rubric

|  | Highly Proficient | Proficient | Developing | % |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| Introduction | Introduction is effectively crafted to grab audience’s attention. Introduction identifies the presentation’s thesis, places the thesis in proper context, and lays out the framework of the presentation. The introduction includes an outline of the overall presentation, briefly describing each part and clarifying fundamental concepts as needed.| Introduction is well-crafted but does not successfully grab and hold audience’s attention. The promised attention-grabber is subsequently underdeveloped or missing content in the body of the presentation. Examples fail to fully connect to the thesis. The introduction includes an outline of all elements of the overall presentation, but without depth. | There is an introduction attempted, but the execution is poor giving the audience no real sense of what to expect from the presentation. Parts missing, such as an outline not being comprehensive or failure to clarify key elements. | 10% |
| Organization | Presentation is well organized and flows smoothly. Transitions are succinct and points are connected. Information is presented in a logical sequence that is easy to follow. Prepared and distributed appropriate handouts/PowerPoints (if applicable). Stayed within the allotted time limits for presentation and Q&A.| Presentation is generally well organized with transitions between key points.  Most of the information was presented in a logical sequence. Handouts not well prepared or not distributed. Appeared to rush or lag to meet time requirements. | Organizational structure is not easily followed by the audience or is not presented in a logical sequence. Transitions be- tween key points are lacking. Required handouts were either missing or poorly done. Did not meet time limit requirements. | 20% |
| Content | Provides an accurate and complete explanation of facts, relevant case law, and other appropriate material. Evaluates existing information and provides a constructive analysis. The argument is clear, logical, and amply supported.| Information is substantially accurate. Some analysis is provided. Evidence is provided based solely on case law. The argument is logical and adequately supported. | Information relied upon contains inaccuracies, mistakes of law and/or mistakes of fact. Key facts may have been omitted. Information not supported by case law. Little or no evaluation or analysis. | 40% |
| Presentation Style | Poised, articulate, appropriate volume, steady pace, uses inflection. Pronunciation is correct and clear. Appropriate word
choice and grammar. Absence of linguistic blunder. Appropriate eye contact, gestures and movement. Refers to notes but does not read from them. Appearance and attire were appropriate. | Articulate but not polished. Word choice and grammar use is mostly correct and appropriate. Speaks in full sentences with minimal hesitation or unnecessary verbal pauses. Flows. Appearance was neat. | Speaker appears unsure or awkward. Volume, pace, or inflection cause presentation to sound choppy. Distracting use of pause words interrupts the flow. Poor eye contact, overuse of hand gesture or movement, or wooden in appearance. Over reliance on written notes. Attire was not appropriate. | 20% |
| Conclusion | Ends with an accurate conclusion, demonstrating a thoughtful, strong evaluation of the evidence presented with insightful observations. No new or confusing information is addressed. Takes and answers questions from the instructor and students appropriately.| Ends with a summary of main points showing some evaluation of the evidence presented. Asks for questions. | Ends with a summary but little or no evaluation.  New or confusing information is presented. Does not ask for questions or provides weak responses to questions. | 10% |
